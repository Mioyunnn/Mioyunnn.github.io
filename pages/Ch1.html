筆記 2024/5/28 : Leda and the Swan

Leda was seduced by Zeus, who had transformed into a swan, and she laid two eggs. Each egg turned into two children. From the first egg, Pollux and Helen were Zeus's children, while from the other egg, Castor and Clytemnestra were the children of Tyndareus.

Helen is the reason that Greece united together.

staggering 步履蹣跚

bill = mouth

"By the dark webs, her nape caught in his bill" = The lady being caught by man in violent way.

Violent sex

Juxtaposition 並置, 強烈反差物體的並列

"How can those terrified vague fingers push" = 給男生一種欲拒還迎的感覺，男生難以判斷究竟是要做還是不要

The term "white rush" in the second stanza refers to the swift, forceful movement of the swan, which is Zeus in disguise. The "white" describes the swan's color, while "rush" conveys the sudden, powerful action of the swan attacking Leda. This phrase encapsulates the intensity and violence of the encounter.

"A shudder in the loins engenders there" = 女性與男性達到高潮的反應, shudder 抖動、loins 腰部、engender 產生

"Did she put on his knowledge with his power" = 肉體被搶走了，但精神有被他(Zeus)統治嗎?

indifferent beak = 簡而言之就是聖人模式，會讓男生有一種漠視的態度

let her drop = 被遺棄

Is the image of the swan positive or negative? Why? Negative, the author describes the swan as a raper. The raper do the sex with a violent way. After he finished, he just abandon her.

In stanzas 2 and 4, the speaker asks three questions. What is their significance? He question about the true thought of the woman.

Discuss the use of present participle(such as "beating", "staggering", "loosening", "burning") in this poem. The author is trying to create the sense of action which happen between the swan and the woman.

筆記 2024/6/4 : The introduction Ode on a Grecian Urn by John Keats

John Keats傾向於描寫在生命達到高潮後趨於平淡(after-climax)的生活。

John Keats的作品，用創新的頌體，但當時不被理解。

他習慣在描述的背加上綠色植物。

searching for bride of quietness

The poet also uses many rhetorical questions in first stanza. (rhetorical questions = 反問句、反詰)

Grecian urns was originally for storaging food, water , wine.

Hellenism = 世俗主義者

apostrophe = 頓呼法(對物品講話)

"In Tempe or the dales of Arcady" idyllic setting = 田園畫

"What men or gods are these? What... ...What wild ecstasy?" =　希臘的狂歡、男女互相追逐求歡。

Ecstacy = 狂歡、情感

Timbrel = 鈴鼓

Second stanza, the youth is the piper and only to keep piping the song.

The tree will not be bold forever. Moment = frozen time = eternity, timelessness.

Bold Lover = young boy, 勇敢去追愛吧，但你永遠追不到，因為你被卡在追逐的過程(frozen, process)。

但女生也永遠不會消失(fade)

追逐的時候是最美的時刻，追到手之後不見得會依然美麗。

Third stanza, happy boughs are people woh can never shed the leaves. 永保青春

happy love = woman, woman keep panting 不斷的喘息

"All breathing human passion far above. A burning forehead, and a parching tongue."" 在講述說mortality 人類，費盡口舌、熱情(parching tongue, passion)只會得到(a heart high-sorrowful and coly'd)而卻不如一個甕。

A comparison of eternity and mortality.

Fourth stanza, sacrifice

decorating the heifer by a mysterious priest.

Apostrophe on "And, little town, thy streets for evermore. Will silent be; and not a soul to tell. Why thou art desolate, can e'er return."

desolate = lonely. because they are frozened in time, cannot come back.

作者把自己譬喻成孤城(simile himself as a empty city/town)，沒人能理解他。

mortals will be decay in time.

"Thou, silent form, dost tease us out of thought." we are be teasd by eternity.

"Cold Pastroal!" 冷漠的田園畫、永恆本身。

This generation of people are keep wasting time.

Sorrow and pain is a friend to man.

"Beauty is truth, truth beauty, -that is all Ye know on earth, and all ye need to know." = 在世界該懂的東西只有美而已。
